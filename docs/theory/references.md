# Academic References

This document contains the complete list of academic references that form the theoretical foundation of the SPAF framework.

## Core References

### Shin Method - True Probability Extraction

**Shin, H.S. (1992).** "Prices of State Contingent Claims with Insider Traders, and the Favourite-Longshot Bias." *The Economic Journal*, 102(411), 426-435.

DOI: [10.2307/2234526](https://doi.org/10.2307/2234526)

> This paper establishes the mathematical framework for extracting "fair" probabilities from bookmaker odds by decomposing them into true probability, margin, and information asymmetry components.

---

### Bayesian Data Analysis

**Gelman, A., Carlin, J.B., Stern, H.S., Dunson, D.B., Vehtari, A. & Rubin, D.B. (2013).** *Bayesian Data Analysis* (3rd ed.). CRC Press.

DOI: [10.1201/b16018](https://doi.org/10.1201/b16018)

> The foundational text for Bayesian inference. Probability flow analysis is conceptually equivalent to Bayesian posterior updating.

---

### Kelly Criterion

**Kelly, J.L. (1956).** "A New Interpretation of Information Rate." *The Bell System Technical Journal*, 35(4), 917-926.

DOI: [10.1002/j.1538-7305.1956.tb03809.x](https://doi.org/10.1002/j.1538-7305.1956.tb03809.x)

> The original paper establishing optimal capital allocation for positive expected value scenarios. Adapted in this framework as a heuristic for signal-strength-based allocation.

---

### Time Series Momentum

**Moskowitz, T.J., Ooi, Y.H. & Pedersen, L.H. (2012).** "Time Series Momentum." *Journal of Financial Economics*, 104(2), 228-250.

DOI: [10.1016/j.jfineco.2011.11.003](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2011.11.003)

> Provides the theoretical basis for the amplification effect - demonstrating that assets with positive momentum tend to continue performing well.

---

### Information Cascade and Herd Behavior

**Banerjee, A.V. (1992).** "A Simple Model of Herd Behavior." *The Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 107(3), 797-817.

DOI: [10.2307/2118364](https://doi.org/10.2307/2118364)

> Explains the behavioral economics foundation for why probability flows cascade through adjacent outcomes.

---

### Prospect Theory

**Kahneman, D. & Tversky, A. (1979).** "Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk." *Econometrica*, 47(2), 263-291.

DOI: [10.2307/1914185](https://doi.org/10.2307/1914185)

> Nobel Prize-winning work on systematic biases in human decision-making. Informs the bias mitigation strategies in the framework.

---

### Modern Portfolio Theory

**Markowitz, H. (1952).** "Portfolio Selection." *The Journal of Finance*, 7(1), 77-91.

DOI: [10.2307/2975974](https://doi.org/10.2307/2975974)

> The foundation of diversification theory. Adapted for scheme design to reduce variance while preserving upside potential.

---

### Prediction Market Prices

**Wolfers, J. & Zitzewitz, E. (2006).** "Interpreting Prediction Market Prices as Probabilities." *NBER Working Paper* No. 12200.

DOI: [10.3386/w12200](https://doi.org/10.3386/w12200)

> Demonstrates that prediction market prices can be interpreted as event probabilities - the direct theoretical basis for probability flow analysis.

---

### Information Efficiency in Betting Markets

**Cain, M., Law, D. & Peel, D. (2000).** "Information and efficiency: an empirical study of a fixed odds betting market." *Applied Economics*, 32(11), 1433-1441.

DOI: [10.1080/00036840050151449](https://doi.org/10.1080/00036840050151449)

> Empirical study showing that odds movements reflect information updates, but markets are not fully efficient.

---

### Favorite-Longshot Bias

**Snowberg, E. & Wolfers, J. (2010).** "Explaining the Favorite-Longshot Bias: Is it Risk-Love or Misperceptions?" *NBER Working Paper* No. 15923.

DOI: [10.3386/w15923](https://doi.org/10.3386/w15923)

> Explains the systematic bias in betting markets that probability flow analysis can exploit.

---

### OODA Loop

**Boyd, J.R. (1987).** "A Discourse on Winning and Losing." *Air University Press* (Briefing Slides).

> The military decision-making framework adapted for the operational architecture of this framework. Originally classified, now widely cited in strategic studies.

---

### The Use of Knowledge in Society

**Hayek, F.A. (1945).** "The Use of Knowledge in Society." *American Economic Review*, 35(4), 519-530.

> Nobel Prize-winning essay on how dispersed information is aggregated through price systems - the economic foundation for interpreting odds movements as information aggregation.

---
